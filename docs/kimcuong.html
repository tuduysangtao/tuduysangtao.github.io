Understanding Problem Space

The Problem Space is where we seek to understand the challenge at hand through research, exploration, and careful definition. It consists of two phases:

  • Divergent thinking: Expand the scope to discover insights by gathering user data, analyzing market trends, and exploring multiple perspectives.
  • Convergent thinking: Narrow down to define the core problem that needs solving, creating a focused problem statement that guides solution development.

This dual approach ensures that we fully understand what we're solving before attempting to solve it.

Understanding Solution Space

The Solution Space is where we develop responses to the defined problem through ideation, prototyping, and refinement. It also consists of two phases:

  • Divergent thinking: Generate many potential solutions through brainstorming, ideation workshops, and creative exploration without judgment.
  • Convergent thinking: Evaluate ideas against criteria, test prototypes, and refine toward an optimal solution that can be implemented.

This approach ensures we consider a wide range of possibilities before committing to a specific solution path.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the Double Diamond different from other problem-solving frameworks?

Unlike linear problem-solving approaches, the Double Diamond explicitly separates problem understanding from solution development, with distinct divergent and convergent thinking phases for each. It emphasizes deep problem exploration before jumping to solutions, helping teams avoid solving the wrong problems.

When should you cycle back in the process?

You should consider cycling back when: solution testing reveals misunderstandings about the problem; new insights emerge that challenge your problem definition; initial solution concepts don't address the core problem effectively; or when the context or requirements change significantly during the process.

How do you balance divergent and convergent thinking?

Balance is achieved by setting clear timeboxes for each phase, establishing criteria for convergence before diverging, involving diverse perspectives in divergent phases, using structured methods to facilitate transitions, and documenting insights throughout to maintain continuity between phases.
