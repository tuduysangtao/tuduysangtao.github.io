About TRIZ
What is TRIZ?

TRIZ (теория решения изобретательских задач, or Theory of Inventive Problem Solving) is a problem-solving methodology based on logic, data, and research, not intuition. It was developed by Genrich Altshuller and his colleagues in the former USSR starting in 1946.

The 40 Principles

The 40 TRIZ principles represent patterns of invention found across many fields. They help solve contradictions — situations where improving one parameter would worsen another.

Principle Relationships

The principles are interconnected, with some naturally complementing or extending others. Exploring related principles can help you find the best solution for your specific problem.
