How to Use the 5 WHYS Technique Effectively
Define the Problem
Start with a clear, specific statement of the problem you're trying to solve. Be factual and focus on what happened, not who is to blame.
Ask "Why?"
Ask why the problem occurred and document the answer based on facts. Keep responses concise and focused on a single cause.
Dig Deeper
For each answer, ask "Why?" again. Continue this process five times to get beyond symptoms to the underlying root cause.
Be Specific
Avoid vague statements. Each answer should be precise and based on evidence rather than assumptions or opinions.
Example: Manufacturing Defect Analysis
Problem Statement
Customer received a product with a broken component
Why #1
The component fractured during shipping
Why #2
The component was too brittle
Why #3
The wrong material was used in manufacturing
Why #4
The material specification was changed without updating QA protocols
Why #5 (Root Cause)
There's no change management process to ensure updates are communicated across teams
